Getting Here


Photo by Celina Reeb, taken in 2022. Mo'Orea.
Image source: "Six Predictions For Summer Air Travel" by Ben Baldanza, Forbes

The majority of visitors arrive in Taniti by air, although a small cruise ship also docks at Yellow Leaf Bay once a week for an overnight stay. The island is currently served by a modest airport, capable of accommodating small jets and propeller planes. Plans are underway to expand the airport, allowing larger jets to land on the island within the next few years, enhancing accessibility for future visitors.


Lodging


Photo by Celina Reeb, taken in 2022. Papeete, Tahiti.

Taniti Island offers a diverse range of lodging options, catering to various preferences and budgets. Accommodations span from affordable hostels to a prestigious four-star resort. The island is also home to numerous small, family-operated hotels and an increasing number of charming bed and breakfasts. All lodging establishments are subject to strict regulations and undergo regular inspections by the Tanitian government, ensuring high standards of quality and safety for all visitors.

Getting Around

Public buses operate within Taniti City from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily, providing convenient transit for visitors. Private bus services cover the rest of the island, offering additional transportation options. Taxis are readily available in Taniti City, and rental cars can be obtained from a local agency near the airport. For those looking for more active transportation, bicycles and helmets are available for rent from several vendors, with helmets being legally required. Taniti City is relatively flat and highly walkable, making it easy to explore on foot. Many visitors choose to stay in the Merriton Landing area, which is particularly pedestrian-friendly.
